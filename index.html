About the Event

MAIN Segment 2025 is a one-day multidisciplinary experience designed to connect mechanical engineering fundamentals with the rising power of AI and Data Science. Whether you're a student, graduate, or professional, this event opens a new dimension of tech-driven innovation and career potential.

Mechanical-Focused Workshops

Explore tutorial-based sessions designed to enhance your mechanical engineering skills. All you need is a laptop!

Six Sigma, Lean Production

+

Learn how data-driven methods and Lean principles are used to improve quality, reduce waste, and boost efficiency in production and engineering processes. Ideal for anyone looking to enhance operational excellence.

Project Management

+

An introduction to project management strategies and tools designed for engineering projects. Topics include planning, scheduling, risk assessment, and effective resource management.

Robotics

+

Explore the fundamentals of robotics theory through guided tutorials. Covers robot design, control systems, and automation basicsâ€”no extra materials required.

CFD with ANSYS Fluent

+

Tutorials on Computational Fluid Dynamics (CFD) using ANSYS Fluent to simulate and analyze fluid flow and thermal behavior in engineering systems, all within a software environment.

How to Prepare for the Interviews

+

Practical advice and walkthroughs for crafting a strong resume, acing engineering interviews, handling technical and behavioral questions, and presenting yourself optimally for career opportunities.

BIM for Mechanical Engineers

+

Learn the essentials of Building Information Modeling (BIM) software as applied to mechanical systems in construction projects, focusing on collaborative workflows and digital 3D modeling.

Supply Chain Basics

+

An overview of supply chain management, including logistics, procurement, inventory control, and optimization techniques relevant to engineering. Sessions focus on practical concepts and real-world scenarios.

Fusion 360 for 3D Printing

+

Guided lessons on using Autodesk Fusion 360 for 3D modeling and preparing designs for additive manufacturing. Emphasizes best digital practices, without any hands-on printing.

AI in Engineering

+

Introduction to artificial intelligence applications in mechanical engineering, such as predictive maintenance, optimization, and smart automation. Focused on conceptual understanding and digital tools.

Simulations with MATLAB

+

Guided tutorials on the use of MATLAB for simulating mechanical systems and analyzing engineering data with algorithms and visualization tools.

R&D (Research & Development)

+

An overview of digital R&D processes in engineering, focusing on innovative problem-solving, conceptual prototyping, and evaluation using readily available software.
